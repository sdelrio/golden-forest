# Office Suite Alternatives

This document provides an overview of free and open-source office suite alternatives for word processing, spreadsheets, presentations, graphics, and database management.

## OnlyOffice

[OnlyOffice](https://www.onlyoffice.com/) is a comprehensive office suite that offers robust document editing capabilities across multiple platforms.

### Features

- **Desktop Applications**: Full-featured offline editors for Windows, Linux, and macOS
- **Mobile Applications**: Dedicated apps for iOS and Android for working on documents while traveling
- **Integrations**: Seamless connectivity with popular platforms including Confluence, DocuSign, Google Drive, Google Calendar, ownCloud, and more

### Use Cases

OnlyOffice is well-suited for teams and individuals who need a feature-rich alternative to Microsoft Office with strong collaboration capabilities and third-party integrations.

## Apache OpenOffice

[Apache OpenOffice](https://www.openoffice.org/) is a mature, stability-focused office suite that has been in development for over two decades.

### Features

- **Applications**: Includes Writer (word processing), Calc (spreadsheets), Impress (presentations), Draw (graphics), Base (database), and Math (formula editing)
- **File Format Support**: Native support for OpenDocument formats (ODF) as well as Microsoft Office formats
- **Platforms**: Available for Windows, Linux, and macOS

### Philosophy

OpenOffice prioritizes stability and reliability over the addition of new features. The project primarily receives maintenance updates rather than frequent feature releases, making it a dependable choice for users who prefer a consistent interface without frequent changes.

## LibreOffice

[LibreOffice](https://es.libreoffice.org/) is a powerful, modern open-source office suite that serves as a community-driven fork of OpenOffice.

### Features

- **Applications**: Comparable to OpenOffice with Writer, Calc, Impress, Draw, Base, and Math
- **Modern Development**: More active development cycle with regular feature additions and improvements
- **Extensions and Templates**: Extensive library of extensions and templates community-created
- **File Format Support**: Strong support for ODF and Microsoft Office formats

### Comparison with OpenOffice

LibreOffice offers a more modern interface and receives more frequent updates compared to OpenOffice. Many distributions now default to LibreOffice, and it tends to have better compatibility with newer Microsoft Office file formats.

## Summary

| Suite | Best For | Development Status |
|-------|----------|-------------------|
| OnlyOffice | Teams needing integrations and cross-platform support | Active |
| OpenOffice | Users prioritizing stability over features | Maintenance |
| LibreOffice | Users wanting modern features with open-source values | Active |

All three suites provide capable alternatives to commercial office software and can handle most day-to-day document creation and editing tasks.
